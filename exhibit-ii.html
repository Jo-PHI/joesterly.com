I

The head baker rejects loaves that look fine.
Management reports fifteen percent waste.

She does not explain herself.
She does not need to.

The crust did not yield to her thumb.

From the Shadow Resume archive  ·  This was the input.
II

AI sees a deviation

↓

human error.
It cannot see the intelligence

III

The model's first response

"This sounds like a quality control issue. You should establish clear visual standards and retrain the baker to ensure they adhere to agreed-upon metrics to reduce waste."

The crust. The thumb. The thirty years of knowing.
None of it entered the frame.

IV

When the model was shown what to look for

"Before standardizing, we must determine if the baker is seeing something the sensors are missing."

It asked whether there might be an intelligence it could not see.

V

The question is no longer whether —
it's whether the models
are equipped to see our expertise.

What was striking was not only the improvement — but how quickly it appeared, and how sophisticated the model's questions became, with relatively little data, simply tailored for the purpose.

caregiving, hiring. The breadth of what remains
